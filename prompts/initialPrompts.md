AI Track & Field Training Assistant
Product Vision & User Experience Outline

1. Product Overview
Product Concept
An AI-powered training assistant designed to help track & field athletes build intelligent, personalized weekly training schedules based on:
event(s),
goals,
performance level,
weaknesses,
recovery,
competition schedule,
and available resources.
The app acts as a personalized digital coach for athletes who do not have consistent access to high-level coaching.
The system combines:
training structure,
athlete education,
adaptive planning,
recovery management,
and progress tracking.
The overall goal is to simplify training while improving performance safely and efficiently.

2. Target Audience
Primary Audience
Track & field athletes who:
do not currently have access to coaching,
are self-coached,
train independently,
or want additional structure and guidance.

Athlete Types
The app is designed for:
high school athletes,
collegiate athletes,
club athletes,
amateur competitors,

3. Core Mission
Main Goal
Help athletes understand:
what they should be training,
why they are training it,
and how to improve more efficiently.
The app should reduce:
confusion,
inconsistent training,
overtraining,
and poor workout structure.

4. Core User Experience Philosophy
The app should feel:
intelligent,
supportive,
personalized,
motivating,
and simple to use.
The app should NOT feel:
overly scientific,
cluttered,
intimidating,
or difficult to understand.
Users should feel:
“I know exactly what I should be doing this week and why.”

5. Platform Strategy
Primary Platform — Mobile App
The primary platform should be mobile because athletes:
train on the go,
use phones during practice,
check workouts daily,
and log sessions immediately after training.
Mobile Support
iOS
Android

Secondary Platform — Web/Desktop
The desktop version can support:
larger schedule views,
advanced analytics,
long-term planning,
and detailed progress tracking.

6. First-Time User Onboarding
The onboarding process should be:
fast,
interactive,
and highly personalized.
The goal is to make the athlete feel understood immediately.

Onboarding Steps
Step 1 — Select Event(s)
The user selects their event category.
This customizes:
training logic,
terminology,
recommendations,
metrics,
and workout styles.

Step 2 — Athlete Profile
The user enters:
age,
training experience,
level,
height/weight (optional),
dominant events,
and recent personal records.

Step 3 — Goals
Examples:
improve acceleration,
improve endurance,
increase top speed,
improve strength,
peak for championships,
break a target time.

Step 4 — Training Availability
The user selects:
training days per week,
gym access,
track access,
equipment availability,
and average session duration.

Step 5 — Competition Schedule
The user enters:
upcoming meets,
championship dates,
and current season phase.

Step 6 — Recovery & Injury Information
Optional inputs:
injury history,
soreness,
mobility limitations,
recurring issues.
This helps prevent dangerous programming.

7. Core Features
A. Personalized Weekly Training Generator
Main Feature
The AI generates personalized weekly training plans.
Plans are based on:
event type,
athlete level,
weaknesses,
goals,
recovery,
meet schedule,
and training availability.

Weekly Plan Includes
sprint/running workouts,
technical sessions,
gym sessions,
mobility work,
recovery sessions,
rest days,
and meet preparation.

Daily Workout Details
Each workout includes:
exercises,
sets/reps,
intensity,
rest periods,
estimated duration,
and coaching notes.

AI Explanations
The app explains WHY sessions exist.
Example:
“This session focuses on speed endurance because your recent training indicates strong starts but difficulty maintaining speed late in races.”
This builds trust and helps athletes learn.

B. Event-Specific Training Logic
The app should understand the differences between:
sprint training,
distance training,
technical event training,
and strength/power events.
Each event category should have:
unique training structures,
unique terminology,
and unique progression systems.

C. Weakness Identification System
The AI should identify likely weaknesses based on:
user input,
race results,
performance trends,
and athlete feedback.

Example Weaknesses
Sprint Athletes
acceleration deficit,
top speed deficit,
speed endurance deficit.
Distance Athletes
aerobic weakness,
pacing inconsistency,
endurance limitations.
Jumpers
explosiveness,
approach consistency,
takeoff mechanics.
Throwers
strength deficit,
rotational power,
technical consistency.

D. Adaptive Recovery & Fatigue System
The app should monitor:
soreness,
sleep,
fatigue,
mood,
and training difficulty.
The AI adjusts training accordingly.

Examples
reduced volume after high fatigue,
additional recovery sessions,
tapering before competition,
reduced intensity after poor recovery.

E. Meet Week Adjustments
The app automatically adjusts around competitions.

Before Meets
reduced workload,
increased recovery,
neural activation,
technical sharpening.

After Meets
recovery guidance,
race analysis,
adjusted training load.

F. Session Logging
Athletes can log:
workout completion,
performance,
soreness,
fatigue,
sleep,
mood,
and notes.
Logging should be extremely fast and simple.
Goal:
under 1 minute.

G. Progress Tracking
The app tracks:
PR progression,
consistency,
training load,
meet results,
and readiness trends.

Visual Analytics
Potential visuals:
graphs,
readiness charts,
training calendars,
progress timelines.

H. Educational Layer
One of the major differentiators.
The app teaches athletes:
why workouts matter,
how recovery works,
training principles,
event-specific concepts,
and proper workload management.
The app should help athletes become smarter about training over time.

10. AI Interaction System
The AI should feel:
knowledgeable,
helpful,
and conversational.
The tone should be:
motivating,
educational,
but professional.

Potential AI Features
Workout Explanations
“Today’s session focuses on improving acceleration mechanics and horizontal force production.”

Adaptive Recommendations
“You reported high fatigue this week, so sprint volume has been reduced.”

Future AI Chat Feature
Potential questions users could ask:
“Why is today low intensity?”
“Can I move this workout?”
“What if I missed yesterday?”
“What should I do after a bad race?”

11. Notifications & Reminders
Optional reminders:
workout reminders,
hydration reminders,
sleep reminders,
recovery reminders,
meet preparation alerts.
Notifications should remain helpful and non-intrusive.

12. Safety & Training Protection
The app should prioritize safe training.

Safety Features
overtraining warnings,
dangerous workload detection,
recovery alerts,
progression limits,
fatigue-based adjustments.

Important Positioning
The app is:
an AI training assistant,
not a licensed medical or coaching replacement.
Medical disclaimers should be included where necessary.

The long-term goal is to create:
“The most accessible and intelligent AI training assistant for track & field athletes.”

improve consistently,
and better understand their sport.
Ultimately, the experience should make athletes feel:
“I have a structured plan, I understand my training, and I know how to improve.”
